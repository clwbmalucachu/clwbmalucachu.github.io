The Welsh learner’s guide to new S4C drama Pen Talar

by Carl Morris on September 13, 2010

Pen Talar is a new 9-part television series on Welsh language TV channel S4C. According to the channel it will “take viewers on an epic 50-year journey as we follow characters from two families in west Wales from the early 1960s to the present day”. If you’ve even dipped into the channel recently you’ve probably seen the trailer.

At last the first episode was screened on Sunday night and it’s now available on S4C Clic (the online catch-up service) with the option of English or Welsh subtitles.

Most reactions on Twitter have been very positive indeed.

S4C have a set of Pen Talar activities for Welsh learners. In my opinion the best and most suited for learners are the clips with script extracts. To be honest I didn’t think the quizzes, while fun for some, will help you learn much Welsh. You could also have a look at the S4C’s archive of learner activities across all programmes.

Finally for such an epic series with so much lavish detail and historical background we bring you PenTalarPedia which is an independent effort to catalogue all the detail in a wiki. In other words it’s a website that looks like Wikipedia and embraces its ethos of a community-run encyclopedia (and indeed runs the same software in the background) yet focuses purely on Pen Talar. You will probably need at least a smidgen of Welsh language knowledge to make headway there – see how you get on. As it’s a community effort don’t be shy if you have something to contribute to the wiki, as other people will happily correct your mistakes. Menna Machreth has written a post on Tu Chwith about her idea behind PenTalarPedia.
