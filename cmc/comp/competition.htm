short story for learners competition 2003

judges: Ifor ap Glyn and Debs Williams
first prize: £200
closing date: 2 June 2003
Welsh version  
conditions of entry  
entry form (Welsh)  

Academi and Clwb Malu Cachu have teamed up to launch the brand new Short Story for Learners Competition 2003. We are looking for original Welsh language micro-literature suitable for adult Welsh learners of beginner to intermediate level.

Entries must be 350 words or less and authors can enter as many stories as they like, on any subject. Winners will be announced in August 2003. Entry forms are available from the Academi or here. (You will need to use Adobe Acrobat Reader to open the entry form.)

Competition entries should use simple grammar, short sentences and easy vocabulary. The winner will receive £200 prize money, and their story will be published in A470, Academi’s magazine, and on the Clwb Malu Cachu web site.

Clwb Malu Cachu’s Suw Charman said "I’m every excited to be working with the Academi on this project. I hope that this competition will not only prompt Welsh authors to consider writing for adult learners, but also encourage learners to explore the wide variety of Welsh literature available.

"Reading is an excellent way for learners to expand their vocabulary and gain confidence with the written language, but often they feel overwhelmed by the sheer amount of time required to read a whole novel. Short stories, on the other hand, provide a gentle introduction to Welsh literature and can act as a ‘gateway’ to longer books."

Peter Finch, The Academi’s Chief Executive, said "This is an excellent example of making literature relevant to the needs of its consumers. Today’s Welsh learners are tomorrow’s Welsh book buyers."

About the judges
Ifor ap Glyn
 Ifor ap Glyn is a poet and raconteur who earns his bread and butter in the broadcasting world with Cwmni Da, Caernarfon. He won the National Eisteddfod crown in Angelsey for the poem Golau yn y Gwyll which discussed, amongst other things, the process of learning Welsh. For a time he ran Welsh classes for adults. Ifor’s name is linked with a number of poetry tours, for example, Cicio’r Ciwcymbyr, Fel yr Hêd y Fran and Taith y Saith Sant, and he is a specialist at presenting his work live.

Debs Williams
Debs Williams started to teach herself Welsh from books and tapes in June 1996. Her passion for Welsh music saw her start the bilingual music magazine Welsh Bands Weekly in April 1997, for which she has interviewed luminaries such as the Super Furry Animals, Catatonia and poet Patrick Jones. Debs has also written articles for Barn and Golwg, and various fanzines. For the last two years, Debs has also been part of the Radio Cymru Gwobrau Roc a Phop judging panel.
