Present/Perfect 
Use with 'yn' for Present tense: 	"I am washing" or "I wash" 
					"Are you washing?" or "Do you wash?"
					"I am not washing" or "I don't wash"

Use with 'wedi' for Perfect tense: 	"I have washed" 
					"Have I washed?"
					"I haven't washed"


Affirmative 
dw i/(wi)
(wyt) ti
mae e
mae hi
dan ni/(d)yn ni
dach chi/(d)ych chi
maen nhw


Interrogative
				'yes'
ydw i?				"wyt/ydych"
(wyt) ti?			"ydw"
ydy e?				"ydy"
ydy hi?			"ydy"
ydan ni?/(yd)yn ni?		"ydych/ydyn"
(y)dach chi?/(yd)ych chi?	"ydw/ydyn"
ydyn nhw?			"ydyn"


Negative
dw i ddim
(dwyt) ti ddim
dydy e ddim/dyw e ddim
dydy hi ddim/dyw hi ddim
(dy)dan ni ddim
dach chi ddim
dydyn nhw ddim

Some Southern areas have a different form for the negative: 
sa i
so ti
so fe
so hi
so ni
so chi
so nhw

or

smo fi
smo ti
smo fe
smo hi
smo ni
smo chi
smo nhw

Thus 'sa i'n meddwl' and 'smo fi'n meddwl' are the same as 'dw i ddim yn meddwl'.
